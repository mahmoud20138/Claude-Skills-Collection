# Trading Fundamentals - Complete Knowledge Base

## 1. Market Structure

### 1.1 Market Types
- **Bull Market**: Sustained upward price movement (20%+ gain from recent low)
- **Bear Market**: Sustained downward price movement (20%+ decline from recent high)
- **Sideways/Range-bound Market**: Price oscillates between support and resistance
- **Choppy Market**: Irregular, unpredictable price movements with no clear trend

### 1.2 Market Participants
| Participant | Role | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Retail Traders | Individual investors | Low individual impact, high collective |
| Institutional Investors | Hedge funds, mutual funds, pension funds | High impact, move markets |
| Market Makers | Provide liquidity | Stabilize spreads |
| High-Frequency Traders (HFT) | Algorithmic micro-second trading | Add liquidity, can cause flash crashes |
| Central Banks | Monetary policy | Massive macro impact |
| Commercial Hedgers | Corporations hedging risk | Stabilize commodity markets |

### 1.3 Market Microstructure
- **Order Book**: Collection of buy/sell orders at various price levels
- **Bid-Ask Spread**: Difference between highest buy and lowest sell price
- **Market Depth**: Volume of orders at each price level
- **Slippage**: Difference between expected and actual execution price
- **Latency**: Time delay in order execution
- **Dark Pools**: Private exchanges for large institutional orders

### 1.4 Order Types
Market Order → Execute immediately at best available price
Limit Order → Execute at specified price or better
Stop Order → Becomes market order when price hits trigger
Stop-Limit Order → Becomes limit order when price hits trigger
Trailing Stop → Stop that follows price by fixed amount/percentage
IOC (Immediate or Cancel) → Fill immediately or cancel unfilled portion
FOK (Fill or Kill) → Fill entirely immediately or cancel completely
GTC (Good Till Cancelled) → Remains active until filled or cancelled
MOO (Market on Open) → Execute at market open price
MOC (Market on Close) → Execute at market close price
Iceberg Order → Large order broken into smaller visible portions
TWAP Order → Time-weighted average price execution
VWAP Order → Volume-weighted average price execution

### 1.5 Asset Classes
- **Equities (Stocks)**: Ownership shares in companies
- **Fixed Income (Bonds)**: Debt instruments with fixed returns
- **Forex (Currencies)**: Currency pair trading
- **Commodities**: Physical goods (gold, oil, wheat)

## 2. Trading Timeframes

### 2.1 Scalping (Seconds to Minutes)
- **Holding Period**: 1 second to 15 minutes
- **Profit Target**: 1-10 pips / 0.01%-0.1%
- **Trades Per Day**: 10-100+
- **Key Tools**: Level 2 data, time & sales, 1-min charts
- **Requirements**: Low latency, low commissions, high liquidity
- **Win Rate Target**: 60-80%
- **Risk:Reward**: Often 1:1 or even 1:0.5 (compensated by high win rate)

### 2.2 Day Trading (Minutes to Hours)
- **Holding Period**: 5 minutes to end of trading day
- **Profit Target**: 10-100 pips / 0.1%-2%
- **Trades Per Day**: 2-15
- **Key Tools**: 5-min, 15-min, 1-hour charts, volume profile
- **Requirements**: Pattern Day Trader rules ($25K minimum in US)
- **Win Rate Target**: 50-65%
- **Risk:Reward**: 1:1.5 to 1:3

### 2.3 Swing Trading (Days to Weeks)
- **Holding Period**: 2-20 trading days
- **Profit Target**: 50-500 pips / 2%-10%
- **Trades Per Week**: 2-10
- **Key Tools**: 1-hour, 4-hour, daily charts
- **Requirements**: Ability to hold overnight
- **Win Rate Target**: 45-60%
- **Risk:Reward**: 1:2 to 1:5

### 2.4 Position Trading (Weeks to Months)
- **Holding Period**: 3 weeks to 6 months
- **Profit Target**: 200-2000+ pips / 10%-50%
- **Trades Per Month**: 1-5
- **Key Tools**: Daily, weekly charts, fundamentals
- **Requirements**: Large capital, patience
- **Win Rate Target**: 40-55%
- **Risk:Reward**: 1:3 to 1:10

### 2.5 Investing (Months to Years)
- **Holding Period**: 6 months to decades
- **Key Tools**: Fundamental analysis, macroeconomics
- **Approach**: Buy and hold, value investing, growth investing

## 3. Core Trading Principles

### 3.1 Risk Management Rules
Rule 1: Never risk more than 1-2% of account per trade
Rule 2: Always use stop losses
Rule 3: Position size based on risk, not conviction
Rule 4: Maximum daily loss limit (typically 3-5% of account)
Rule 5: Maximum weekly loss limit (typically 5-10%)
Rule 6: Maximum drawdown limit (typically 15-25%)
Rule 7: Correlation risk - don't overexpose to same sector/direction
Rule 8: Risk:Reward minimum 1:1.5 for most strategies
Rule 9: Never add to losing positions (unless strategy specifically calls for it)
Rule 10: Reduce position size during losing streaks

### 3.2 Position Sizing Formulas

#### Fixed Percentage Risk Model
Position Size = (Account Balance × Risk%) / (Entry Price - Stop Loss)

Example:
Account: $100,000
Risk: 1% = $1,000
Entry: $50.00
Stop Loss: $48.00
Risk per share: $2.00
Position Size: $1,000 / $2.00 = 500 shares

text


#### Kelly Criterion
Kelly % = W - [(1-W) / R]
Where:
W = Win rate (decimal)
R = Average Win / Average Loss

Example:
Win Rate: 55% (0.55)
Avg Win: $300, Avg Loss: $200
R = 300/200 = 1.5
Kelly% = 0.55 - (0.45/1.5) = 0.55 - 0.30 = 0.25 (25%)

Note: Most traders use "Half Kelly" (12.5%) for safety

text


#### Volatility-Based Position Sizing (ATR Method)
Position Size = (Account × Risk%) / (ATR × ATR Multiplier)

Example:
Account: $100,000
Risk: 1% = $1,000
14-period ATR: $3.50
ATR Multiplier: 2
Position Size: $1,000 / ($3.50 × 2) = 142 shares

text


### 3.3 Risk-Reward Analysis
Expected Value = (Win Rate × Average Win) - (Loss Rate × Average Loss)

Example:
Win Rate: 45%
Average Win: $500
Loss Rate: 55%
Average Loss: $200
EV = (0.45 × $500) - (0.55 × $200) = $225 - $110 = +$115 per trade

Profit Factor = Gross Profits / Gross Losses
Target: > 1.5 (good), > 2.0 (excellent)

text


### 3.4 Drawdown Management
Maximum Drawdown = (Peak Value - Trough Value) / Peak Value × 100

Recovery needed:
10% drawdown → need 11.1% gain to recover
20% drawdown → need 25% gain to recover
30% drawdown → need 42.9% gain to recover
50% drawdown → need 100% gain to recover
75% drawdown → need 300% gain to recover

## 4. Trading Psychology

### 4.1 Cognitive Biases in Trading
| Bias | Description | Counter-Measure |
|---|---|---|
| Confirmation Bias | Seeking info that confirms existing belief | Actively seek opposing views |
| Loss Aversion | Feeling losses 2x more than equivalent gains | Pre-defined stop losses |
| Recency Bias | Overweighting recent events | Use statistical analysis |
| Anchoring | Fixating on specific price points | Focus on current market conditions |
| Overconfidence | Overestimating ability after wins | Track statistics objectively |
| Gambler's Fallacy | Believing past outcomes affect future | Each trade is independent |
| Disposition Effect | Selling winners too early, holding losers | Trail stops on winners |
| Hindsight Bias | Believing past events were predictable | Keep a trading journal |
| Sunk Cost Fallacy | Holding losers because of prior investment | Focus on future potential only |
| Herding | Following crowd without analysis | Independent analysis first |
| FOMO | Fear of missing out on moves | Stick to trading plan |
| Revenge Trading | Trading emotionally after a loss | Mandatory cool-off periods |

### 4.2 Emotional Management Framework
Before Trading:
✓ Check emotional state (1-10 scale)
✓ Review trading plan
✓ Set daily loss limits
✓ Identify key levels and scenarios
✓ Physical: hydrated, rested, fed

During Trading:
✓ Follow the plan exactly
✓ No impulsive decisions
✓ Step away if emotional (5-minute rule)
✓ Breathe deeply before executing
✓ Accept uncertainty

After Trading:
✓ Journal every trade
✓ Review emotions felt
✓ Calculate statistics
✓ Identify improvement areas
✓ Celebrate process, not outcomes

## 3. Technical Indicators

### 3.1 Trend Indicators

#### Moving Averages
Simple Moving Average (SMA):
Formula: SMA = (P1 + P2 + ... + Pn) / n
Common Periods: 10, 20, 50, 100, 200
Usage: Trend direction, dynamic support/resistance

Exponential Moving Average (EMA):
Formula: EMA = Price × k + EMA(previous) × (1-k)
Where k = 2 / (n+1)
More weight to recent prices
Common Periods: 9, 12, 21, 26, 50, 200

Weighted Moving Average (WMA):
Formula: WMA = (P1×n + P2×(n-1) + ... + Pn×1) / (n×(n+1)/2)

Hull Moving Average (HMA):
Formula: HMA = WMA(2×WMA(n/2) - WMA(n), √n)
Reduces lag significantly

Double EMA (DEMA):
Formula: DEMA = 2×EMA(n) - EMA(EMA(n))

Triple EMA (TEMA):
Formula: TEMA = 3×EMA - 3×EMA(EMA) + EMA(EMA(EMA))

Key Moving Average Strategies:

Golden Cross: 50 SMA crosses above 200 SMA → Bullish
Death Cross: 50 SMA crosses below 200 SMA → Bearish
Price above 200 SMA → Long-term bullish
Price below 200 SMA → Long-term bearish
MA Ribbon: Multiple MAs for trend strength visualization
text


#### MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
Components:
MACD Line = 12-EMA - 26-EMA
Signal Line = 9-EMA of MACD Line
Histogram = MACD Line - Signal Line

Signals:
Bullish: MACD crosses above signal line
Bearish: MACD crosses below signal line
Bullish Divergence: Price makes lower low, MACD makes higher low
Bearish Divergence: Price makes higher high, MACD makes lower high
Zero Line Cross: Trend direction confirmation
Histogram expansion: Momentum increasing
Histogram contraction: Momentum decreasing

Best Practices:

Use on daily/4H charts for swing trading
Combine with trend direction
Divergences are strongest signals
Don't use in choppy/ranging markets
text


#### ADX (Average Directional Index)
Components:
+DI (Positive Directional Indicator)
-DI (Negative Directional Indicator)
ADX Line (trend strength, 0-100)

Interpretation:
ADX < 20: Weak trend / No trend (range-bound)
ADX 20-25: Emerging trend
ADX 25-50: Strong trend
ADX 50-75: Very strong trend
ADX > 75: Extremely strong (rare, watch for exhaustion)

Signals:
+DI crosses above -DI: Bullish signal
-DI crosses above +DI: Bearish signal
ADX rising: Trend strengthening
ADX falling: Trend weakening

text


#### Parabolic SAR
Formula: SAR(n+1) = SAR(n) + AF × (EP - SAR(n))
AF = Acceleration Factor (starts 0.02, max 0.20)
EP = Extreme Point

Usage:

Dots below price → Uptrend (go long)
Dots above price → Downtrend (go short)
Dot flip → Potential reversal signal
Excellent for trailing stop placement
Limitations:

Whipsaws in sideways markets
Works best in trending markets
Combine with trend filter (ADX)
text


#### Ichimoku Cloud (Ichimoku Kinko Hyo)
Components:
Tenkan-sen (Conversion Line) = (9-period high + 9-period low) / 2
Kijun-sen (Base Line) = (26-period high + 26-period low) / 2
Senkou Span A (Leading Span A) = (Tenkan + Kijun) / 2, plotted 26 periods ahead
Senkou Span B (Leading Span B) = (52-period high + 52-period low) / 2, plotted 26 ahead
Chikou Span (Lagging Span) = Close plotted 26 periods behind

Cloud (Kumo) = Area between Senkou Span A and B

Signals:
STRONG BULLISH:
- Price above cloud
- Tenkan above Kijun
- Chikou above price from 26 periods ago
- Cloud is green (Span A above Span B)
- Future cloud is green and expanding

STRONG BEARISH:
- Price below cloud
- Tenkan below Kijun
- Chikou below price from 26 periods ago
- Cloud is red (Span B above Span A)
- Future cloud is red and expanding

TK Cross (Tenkan crosses Kijun):
Above cloud → Strong buy
Inside cloud → Neutral
Below cloud → Weak buy

Kumo Breakout:
Price breaks above cloud → Buy
Price breaks below cloud → Sell

Cloud Twist:
Cloud changes from red to green → Bullish shift
Cloud changes from green to red → Bearish shift

Support/Resistance:
Cloud acts as dynamic S/R
Thin cloud = weak S/R, easy to break
Thick cloud = strong S/R, hard to break

text


### 3.2 Momentum Indicators

#### RSI (Relative Strength Index)
Formula: RSI = 100 - (100 / (1 + RS))
RS = Average Gain / Average Loss (over 14 periods default)

Interpretation:
RSI > 70: Overbought (potential sell/short)
RSI < 30: Oversold (potential buy/long)
RSI = 50: Neutral midline

Advanced Usage:
Bullish Divergence: Price lower low + RSI higher low → Buy signal
Bearish Divergence: Price higher high + RSI lower high → Sell signal
Hidden Bullish Divergence: Price higher low + RSI lower low → Trend continuation
Hidden Bearish Divergence: Price lower high + RSI higher high → Trend continuation

Failure Swings:
Bullish: RSI falls below 30, bounces, pulls back (stays above 30), breaks above prior RSI high
Bearish: RSI rises above 70, pulls back, bounces (stays below 70), breaks below prior RSI low

Range Shift:
Bull market RSI range: 40-80
Bear market RSI range: 20-60

RSI Trendlines:
Draw trendlines on RSI itself
Breaks can precede price breaks

text


#### Stochastic Oscillator
Formula:
%K = ((Close - Lowest Low(n)) / (Highest High(n) - Lowest Low(n))) × 100
%D = SMA(%K, 3) [Signal line]
Default period: 14

Types:
Fast Stochastic: Raw %K and %D (very sensitive)
Slow Stochastic: Smoothed %K (SMA of Fast %K) and %D
Full Stochastic: Customizable smoothing

Interpretation:
Above 80: Overbought
Below 20: Oversold
%K crosses above %D below 20: Buy signal
%K crosses below %D above 80: Sell signal

Divergences work same as RSI divergences

text


#### CCI (Commodity Channel Index)
Formula: CCI = (Typical Price - SMA(TP)) / (0.015 × Mean Deviation)
Typical Price = (High + Low + Close) / 3

Interpretation:
CCI > +100: Overbought / Strong uptrend
CCI < -100: Oversold / Strong downtrend
CCI > +200: Extremely overbought
CCI < -200: Extremely oversold
Zero line crossover: Trend direction signal

text


#### Williams %R
Formula: %R = (Highest High - Close) / (Highest High - Lowest Low) × (-100)
Default period: 14

Interpretation:
-20 to 0: Overbought
-100 to -80: Oversold
Essentially inverted Stochastic

text


#### Rate of Change (ROC) / Momentum
ROC = ((Current Price - Price n periods ago) / Price n periods ago) × 100
Momentum = Current Price - Price n periods ago

Usage: Measure speed of price change
Zero line cross = directional signal
Divergences signal potential reversals

text


### 3.3 Volume Indicators

#### On-Balance Volume (OBV)
Formula:
If Close > Previous Close: OBV = Previous OBV + Volume
If Close < Previous Close: OBV = Previous OBV - Volume
If Close = Previous Close: OBV = Previous OBV

Interpretation:
Rising OBV: Buying pressure (volume flowing in)
Falling OBV: Selling pressure (volume flowing out)
OBV divergence from price: Potential reversal
OBV breakout before price breakout: Leading signal

text


#### Volume Weighted Average Price (VWAP)
Formula: VWAP = Σ(Price × Volume) / Σ(Volume)
Calculated from market open, resets daily

Usage:
Price above VWAP: Bullish intraday bias
Price below VWAP: Bearish intraday bias
VWAP acts as dynamic support/resistance
Institutional benchmark for execution quality

Bands: Standard deviation bands around VWAP
+1 SD: Take profit long / Short entry
-1 SD: Take profit short / Long entry
+2 SD: Extreme overbought intraday
-2 SD: Extreme oversold intraday

text


#### Accumulation/Distribution Line (A/D)
Formula:
Money Flow Multiplier = ((Close - Low) - (High - Close)) / (High - Low)
Money Flow Volume = MFM × Volume
A/D = Previous A/D + Money Flow Volume

Interpretation:
Rising A/D + Rising Price: Confirmed uptrend
Falling A/D + Falling Price: Confirmed downtrend
Rising A/D + Falling Price: Bullish divergence (accumulation)
Falling A/D + Rising Price: Bearish divergence (distribution)

text


#### Money Flow Index (MFI)
Formula: MFI = 100 - (100 / (1 + Money Flow Ratio))
"Volume-weighted RSI"

Interpretation:
MFI > 80: Overbought
MFI < 20: Oversold
Divergences: Same as RSI
More reliable than RSI because includes volume

text


#### Chaikin Money Flow (CMF)
Formula: CMF = Sum(Money Flow Volume, 20) / Sum(Volume, 20)

Interpretation:
CMF > 0: Buying pressure (bullish)
CMF < 0: Selling pressure (bearish)
Increasing CMF: Strengthening pressure
Range: -1 to +1

text


#### Volume Profile
Concepts:
Point of Control (POC): Price level with highest traded volume
Value Area High (VAH): Upper boundary of 70% volume area
Value Area Low (VAL): Lower boundary of 70% volume area
High Volume Nodes (HVN): Areas of high trading activity (S/R)
Low Volume Nodes (LVN): Areas of low activity (price moves fast through)

Usage:
POC acts as magnet / support/resistance
Price tends to return to POC
LVNs create "gaps" price moves quickly through
HVNs create congestion zones

Trading Rules:
Open inside Value Area → Expect mean reversion to POC
Open outside Value Area → Expect trend / test of VA edge
Rejection at VAH → Short opportunity
Rejection at VAL → Long opportunity

text


### 3.4 Volatility Indicators

#### Bollinger Bands
Formula:
Middle Band = 20-period SMA
Upper Band = Middle Band + (2 × Standard Deviation)
Lower Band = Middle Band - (2 × Standard Deviation)

Interpretation:
Price at Upper Band: Potentially overbought (not automatic sell)
Price at Lower Band: Potentially oversold (not automatic buy)
Band Squeeze: Low volatility → Expect breakout
Band Expansion: High volatility → Trend in progress

Strategies:
Bollinger Bounce: Buy at lower band, sell at upper band (ranging markets)
Bollinger Squeeze: Trade breakout direction after squeeze
Walking the Band: Strong trend rides upper/lower band

%B Indicator:
%B = (Price - Lower Band) / (Upper Band - Lower Band)
%B > 1: Price above upper band
%B < 0: Price below lower band

Bandwidth:
BW = (Upper Band - Lower Band) / Middle Band
Low BW: Squeeze forming
High BW: High volatility

## 5. Elliott Wave Theory
Basic Structure:
Impulse Wave (5 waves in trend direction):
Wave 1: Initial move (often hard to identify in real-time)
Wave 2: Correction (cannot retrace beyond start of Wave 1)
Wave 3: Strongest & longest (cannot be shortest of 1,3,5)
Wave 4: Correction (cannot overlap Wave 1 territory)
Wave 5: Final push (often with divergence)

Corrective Wave (3 waves against trend):
Wave A: Counter-trend move
Wave B: Partial retracement of A
Wave C: Final counter-trend move

Rules (Must be followed):

Wave 2 cannot retrace more than 100% of Wave 1
Wave 3 cannot be the shortest impulse wave
Wave 4 cannot overlap with Wave 1 price territory
Guidelines (Usually followed):

Wave 2 retraces 50-61.8% of Wave 1
Wave 3 often extends 161.8% of Wave 1
Wave 4 retraces 38.2% of Wave 3
Wave 5 often equals Wave 1 in length
Alternation: If Wave 2 is sharp, Wave 4 is flat (and vice versa)
Corrective Patterns:
Zigzag (5-3-5): Sharp correction
Flat (3-3-5): Sideways correction
Triangle (3-3-3-3-3): Converging correction
Complex: Combinations of above (WXY, WXYXZ)

Fibonacci Relationships:
Wave 2 retracement: 0.500, 0.618, 0.786 of Wave 1
Wave 3 extension: 1.618, 2.618 of Wave 1
Wave 4 retracement: 0.236, 0.382, 0.500 of Wave 3
Wave 5 extension: 0.618, 1.000 of Wave 1
Wave C: 1.000, 1.618 of Wave A

## 7. Market Profile / Auction Market Theory
Key Concepts:
Initial Balance (IB): First hour of trading range
Value Area: Where 70% of trading occurred (1 StdDev)
Point of Control (POC): Most traded price
Single Prints: Prices with minimal trading (potential targets)

Day Types:
Normal Day: 85% of activity within IB (range-bound)
Normal Variation: Extends beyond IB once, limited extension
Trend Day: Steady directional movement, multiple extensions
Double Distribution: Two distinct value areas
P-Shape: Buying activity, rally then balance (bullish exhaustion)
b-Shape: Selling activity, drop then balance (bearish exhaustion)

Trading Rules:
Open within Value Area → Mean reversion likely
Open outside Value Area → Trend/acceptance at new level
POC migration → Trend direction
Narrow profiles → Breakout imminent
Wide profiles → Consolidation/balance

### 9.4 Options Greeks
Delta (Δ):
Rate of change in option price per $1 move in underlying
Calls: 0 to +1.0 (ATM ≈ 0.50)
Puts: -1.0 to 0 (ATM ≈ -0.50)
Also approximates probability of expiring ITM

Uses:
Position delta = Delta × Number of contracts × 100
Delta-neutral: Total position delta = 0

Gamma (Γ):
Rate of change in delta per $1 move in underlying
Highest for ATM options near expiration
Measures acceleration of delta change

Long gamma: Benefits from large moves (long options)
Short gamma: Benefits from small moves (short options)

Theta (Θ):
Time decay: Dollar amount option loses per day
Always negative for long options
Accelerates as expiration approaches
ATM options have highest theta

Rule of thumb: Options lose 1/3 of value in first 2/3 of life
and 2/3 of value in last 1/3 of life

Vega (ν):
Sensitivity to implied volatility change
Dollar change per 1% change in IV
Highest for ATM, longer-dated options

Long vega: Benefits from IV increase (long options)
Short vega: Benefits from IV decrease (short options)

Rho (ρ):
Sensitivity to interest rate changes
Usually minor impact except for long-dated options

Key Relationships:
Long Calls: +Delta, +Gamma, -Theta, +Vega
Long Puts: -Delta, +Gamma, -Theta, +Vega
Short Calls: -Delta, -Gamma, +Theta, -Vega
Short Puts: +Delta, -Gamma, +Theta, -Vega

text


### 9.5 Implied Volatility Strategies

Where current IV stands relative to its range over past year
IV Rank = (Current IV - 52wk Low IV) / (52wk High IV - 52wk Low IV)

IV Rank > 50%: Consider selling premium (short strategies)
IV Rank < 50%: Consider buying premium (long strategies)

IV Percentile:
Percentage of days in past year that IV was lower than current

IV Percentile > 80%: High IV → Sell premium
IV Percentile < 20%: Low IV → Buy premium

Volatility Smile/Skew:
OTM puts typically have higher IV than OTM calls (equity markets)
Due to crash protection demand
Skew can provide trading signals when extreme

## 10. Pairs Trading / Statistical Arbitrage

### 10.1 Correlation-Based Pairs Trading
Process:

Find two highly correlated assets (correlation > 0.80)
Examples: KO/PEP, XOM/CVX, GLD/SLV, GOOG/META

Calculate the spread:
Spread = Price_A - (Beta × Price_B)
Beta = regression coefficient

Calculate Z-Score of spread:
Z = (Spread - Mean(Spread)) / StdDev(Spread)

Trading Rules:
Z > +2.0: Short A, Long B (expect spread to narrow)
Z < -2.0: Long A, Short B
Z returns to 0: Close both positions
Z > +3.0: Stop loss (spread diverging, correlation breaking)

Position Sizing:
Dollar-neutral: Equal dollar amounts each side
Beta-neutral: Adjust for beta relationship

Advantages:

Market-neutral (hedged)
Profits from relative, not absolute movement
Lower drawdowns
Risks:

Correlation breakdown (regime change)
One stock gaps on news (M&A, bankruptcy)
Convergence may not happen in your timeframe
text


### 10.2 Cointegration-Based Pairs Trading
Concept: Cointegration ≠ Correlation
Correlation: Prices move together
Cointegration: Spread between prices is mean-reverting

Testing for Cointegration:

Engle-Granger Test
Johansen Test
Phillips-Ouliaris Test
Process:

Test pairs for cointegration (p-value < 0.05)
Estimate the cointegrating vector (hedge ratio)
Calculate residual spread
Trade mean reversion of spread (same Z-score rules)
More robust than correlation-based approach
Spread is stationary → True mean reversion

text

## 15. Portfolio Strategies

### 15.1 Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT)
Efficient Frontier:
Maximize return for given risk level

Portfolio Return = Σ(wi × ri)
Portfolio Risk = √(Σ Σ wi × wj × σi × σj × ρij)

Where:
wi = weight of asset i
ri = expected return of asset i
σi = standard deviation of asset i
ρij = correlation between assets i and j

Sharpe Ratio = (Portfolio Return - Risk-Free Rate) / Portfolio StdDev

1.0: Good
2.0: Very good
3.0: Excellent

Sortino Ratio = (Return - Risk-Free) / Downside Deviation
Only penalizes downside volatility (better for asymmetric returns)

### 15.2 Risk Parity Strategy
Concept: Allocate based on risk contribution, not capital

Each asset contributes equally to portfolio risk

Steps:

Calculate volatility (σ) for each asset
Weight inversely to volatility
wi ∝ 1/σi
Leverage to target desired return
Example:
Stocks σ = 15%, Bonds σ = 5%, Commodities σ = 12%
Inverse: 1/15 = 0.067, 1/5 = 0.200, 1/12 = 0.083
Normalize: Stocks 19%, Bonds 57%, Commodities 24%

Use leverage to achieve equity-like returns with bond-like risk

< 2.0: Low leverage
2.0-3.0: Moderate

4.0: High (potential concern)

Interest Coverage = EBIT / Interest Expense

5×: Very comfortable
3-5×: Adequate
< 2×: Potential distress

Net Debt = Total Debt - Cash
Net Debt/EBITDA: Most common leverage metric
Negative: Net cash position (very strong)

## 3. Valuation Methods

### 3.1 Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
Formula:
Intrinsic Value = Σ [FCFt / (1+r)^t] + Terminal Value / (1+r)^n

Steps:

Project Free Cash Flow for 5-10 years
Calculate Terminal Value (value beyond projection period)
Discount all cash flows to present value
Sum = Enterprise Value
Subtract debt, add cash = Equity Value
Divide by shares outstanding = Per share value
Terminal Value Methods:
Gordon Growth: TV = FCF(n+1) / (r - g)
g = long-term growth rate (usually 2-3%, GDP growth)
Exit Multiple: TV = EBITDA(n) × Exit EV/EBITDA multiple

Discount Rate (WACC):
WACC = (E/V × Re) + (D/V × Rd × (1-T))

Re = Cost of equity (CAPM: Rf + β × (Rm - Rf))
Rd = Cost of debt (interest rate)
E = Market value of equity
D = Market value of debt
V = E + D
T = Tax rate

Typical WACC: 8-12% for most companies

Sensitivity Analysis:
Always run DCF with multiple scenarios:
- Bull case (high growth, low WACC)
- Base case (moderate assumptions)
- Bear case (low growth, high WACC)

Key sensitivities:
- Growth rate assumptions
- WACC (discount rate)
- Terminal growth rate
- Margin assumptions

Process:

Apply median/average multiples to target company
Derive implied valuation

EV/EBITDA: Most common

### 3.3 Dividend Discount Model (DDM)
Gordon Growth Model:
Value = D1 / (r - g)
D1 = Next year's expected dividend
r = Required rate of return
g = Dividend growth rate

Only works when g < r

Two-Stage DDM:
Stage 1: High growth period (5-10 years)
Stage 2: Stable growth in perpetuity

Value = Σ [Dt / (1+r)^t] + [Dn+1 / (r-g)] / (1+r)^n

H-Model (Transitional):
Value = D0 × (1+gL) / (r-gL) + D0 × H × (gS-gL) / (r-gL)
H = Half-life of high-growth period
gS = Short-term growth, gL = Long-term growth

Dividend Safety:
Payout Ratio = Dividends / Earnings (< 60% preferred)
FCF Payout Ratio = Dividends / FCF (< 70% preferred)
Dividend Coverage = EPS / DPS (> 1.5× preferred)
Dividend Growth History: Consistent raises for 10+ years

# Quantitative & Algorithmic Trading Strategies

## 1. Statistical Foundations

### 1.1 Key Statistical Concepts
Descriptive Statistics:
Mean (μ): Average value
Median: Middle value
Mode: Most frequent value
Standard Deviation (σ): Measure of dispersion
Variance (σ²): Square of standard deviation
Skewness: Asymmetry of distribution
Positive skew: Right tail longer (more extreme gains)
Negative skew: Left tail longer (more extreme losses)
Kurtosis: "Fatness" of tails
Leptokurtic (>3): Fat tails (more extreme events than normal)
Mesokurtic (=3): Normal distribution
Platykurtic (<3): Thin tails

Financial Returns Distribution:
NOT normally distributed
Fat tails (leptokurtic) - extreme events more common
Slightly negative skew (crashes more severe than rallies)
Volatility clustering (GARCH effects)

### 1.2 Time Series Analysis
Stationarity:
Stationary series: Constant mean, variance, autocorrelation
Most price series are NON-stationary
Returns (log returns) are approximately stationary

Tests: Augmented Dickey-Fuller (ADF), KPSS, Phillips-Perron

Autocorrelation:
ACF (Autocorrelation Function): Correlation of series with lagged version
PACF (Partial ACF): Direct correlation (removing intermediate effects)

Significant autocorrelation → Predictable component exists

ARIMA Models:
AR(p): Autoregressive - past values predict future
I(d): Integrated - differencing for stationarity
MA(q): Moving Average - past forecast errors predict future

ARIMA(p,d,q): Combine all three

Process:
1. Check stationarity (ADF test)
2. Difference if needed (d parameter)
3. Plot ACF/PACF to determine p and q
4. Fit model, check residuals
5. Forecast

GARCH Models (Volatility):
GARCH(1,1): σ²t = ω + α×ε²(t-1) + β×σ²(t-1)

Captures volatility clustering
Used for: Options pricing, risk management, position sizing

Variants:
EGARCH: Asymmetric (leverage effect)
GJR-GARCH: Asymmetric with threshold
TGARCH: Threshold GARCH

## 3. Backtesting Framework

### 3.1 Backtesting Best Practices
Critical Rules:

No look-ahead bias (only use data available at time of signal)
Include transaction costs (commissions, spreads, slippage)
Account for survivorship bias (include delisted stocks)
Use point-in-time data (not restated financials)
Walk-forward analysis (not just in-sample)
Out-of-sample testing
Realistic execution assumptions
Account for market impact (large orders)
Multiple time periods
Statistical significance tests
Walk-Forward Analysis:

Train on Period 1 → Test on Period 2
Train on Periods 1-2 → Test on Period 3
Train on Periods 1-3 → Test on Period 4
... continue
Only use out-of-sample results for performance assessment

Monte Carlo Simulation:
Randomize trade order to assess range of possible outcomes
Bootstrap returns to estimate confidence intervals
Assess probability of different drawdown levels

### 3.2 Performance Metrics
Return Metrics:
Total Return: (End Value - Start Value) / Start Value
CAGR: (End/Start)^(1/Years) - 1
Annualized Return: Average daily return × 252

Risk Metrics:
Annualized Volatility: Daily StdDev × √252
Maximum Drawdown: Largest peak-to-trough decline
Average Drawdown: Mean of all drawdowns
Drawdown Duration: Time in drawdown

Risk-Adjusted:
Sharpe Ratio: (Return - Rf) / Volatility
Sortino Ratio: (Return - Rf) / Downside Deviation
Calmar Ratio: CAGR / Max Drawdown
Information Ratio: Alpha / Tracking Error
Omega Ratio: Probability-weighted gain / Probability-weighted loss

Trade Statistics:
Win Rate: Winning trades / Total trades
Average Win: Mean profit on winning trades
Average Loss: Mean loss on losing trades
Win/Loss Ratio: Average Win / Average Loss
Profit Factor: Gross Profits / Gross Losses
Expectancy: (Win% × Avg Win) - (Loss% × Avg Loss)
Maximum Consecutive Wins/Losses
Average Holding Period
Trades per Month/Year

R-Multiple Distribution:
R = Risk unit (amount risked per trade)
Track each trade outcome in R-multiples
Average R: Should be positive
R-Expectancy: Key metric for system quality

System Quality Number (SQN):
SQN = √N × Average R / StdDev of R
< 1.6: Poor
1.6-1.9: Below average
2.0-2.4: Average
2.5-2.9: Good
3.0-5.0: Excellent
5.0-6.9: Superb
> 7.0: Holy Grail (very rare)

### 3.3 Common Backtesting Pitfalls
Overfitting:

Too many parameters
Fitting noise, not signal
Solution: Regularization, fewer parameters, out-of-sample testing
Survivorship Bias:

Only testing stocks that exist today
Ignoring delisted/bankrupt companies
Solution: Use survivorship-bias-free databases
Look-Ahead Bias:

Using future information in current decisions
Example: Using revised earnings data instead of original
Solution: Use point-in-time data, careful coding
Selection Bias:

Cherry-picking favorable time periods
Testing on same data used to develop strategy
Solution: True out-of-sample testing, multiple periods
Market Impact:

Assuming unlimited liquidity
Not accounting for price movement from own orders
Solution: Model market impact, limit position sizes
Transaction Cost Underestimation:

Not including all costs (commissions, spread, slippage, taxes)
Solution: Use conservative cost estimates
Regime Change:

Past relationships may not hold in future
Solution: Test across multiple market regimes, adaptive systems
Data Snooping:

Testing many hypotheses, some will work by chance
Solution: Bonferroni correction, White's Reality Check,
Hansen's SPA test

## 4. Algorithmic Execution Strategies

### 4.1 Execution Algorithms
TWAP (Time-Weighted Average Price):
Split large order into equal parts over time
Execute at regular intervals
Benchmark: Average price over execution period
Best for: Low urgency, thin markets

VWAP (Volume-Weighted Average Price):
Split order based on historical volume profile
Execute more during high-volume periods
Benchmark: VWAP of execution period
Best for: Large orders, matching market participation

Implementation Shortfall (IS):
Minimize difference between decision price and execution price
More aggressive at start (front-loaded)
Balances market impact vs. timing risk
Best for: Alpha-driven, time-sensitive orders

Iceberg:
Show only small portion of total order
Replenish as fills occur
Best for: Very large orders, avoiding information leakage

Percentage of Volume (POV):
Participate at fixed percentage of market volume
Adapts to market activity
Best for: Matching market participation rate
Typical: 5-20% of volume

Sniper / Opportunistic:
Wait for favorable price, execute aggressively
Use limit orders at target prices
Best for: Patient, price-sensitive orders

Dark Pool Aggregator:
Route to multiple dark pools for anonymous execution
Best for: Large institutional orders
